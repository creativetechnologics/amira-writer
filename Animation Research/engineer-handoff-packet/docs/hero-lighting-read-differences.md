# 72 — Hero Lighting Read Differences

Date: 2026-03-31

## Purpose
Record the most important predictable differences in how Luke and Amira should read under the same location lighting.

## Luke Hart
Typical lighting sensitivities:
- medic/desert costume needs fold and pocket readability preserved
- face can tolerate slightly stronger shadow shaping than Amira in many scenes
- satchel / straps can help preserve silhouette under wider or harsher light
- fluorescent lighting should not wash out fabric hierarchy

## Amira Nazari
Typical lighting sensitivities:
- face framing and scarf/hair silhouette need stronger protection
- warm sunset profiles should preserve elegance without over-saturating skin/clothing
- moonlit and mixed practical night scenes need careful eye and mouth readability
- ceremony fabrics may react more visibly to hue shifts than Luke's field costume

## Shared rule
Both characters must remain part of the same light world, but not at the cost of flattening their distinct material and silhouette behavior.

## Practical comparison dimensions
For each paired pilot bundle, compare:
- face readability
- mouth readability
- costume readability
- silhouette separation
- practical-light reaction
- likely routing risk
